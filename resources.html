Frequently Asked Questions

What is Fiat Money?
Fiat money is government-issued currency that isn't backed by a physical commodity like gold or silver. Its value comes from the trust people place in the government that issues it. Examples include the US Dollar, Euro, and British Pound. Unlike Bitcoin, fiat money can be printed indefinitely, which can lead to inflation.
How Does Bitcoin Differ from Traditional Money?
Bitcoin is decentralized, meaning no single entity (like a government or bank) controls it. It operates on a blockchain, a public ledger that records all transactions. Unlike traditional money, Bitcoin has a fixed supply of 21 million coins, making it immune to inflation caused by overprinting.

Bitcoin Terminology

Diving into the world of Bitcoin and cryptocurrency can be overwhelming with its unique jargon. To help you navigate this exciting landscape, we've compiled a list of essential terms related to Bitcoin, cybersecurity, and investing, presented in an easy-to-understand manner.

Bitcoin and Cryptocurrency Terms
Bitcoin (BTC)
The first and most well-known cryptocurrency, introduced in 2009 by an anonymous entity known as Satoshi Nakamoto. Bitcoin operates on a decentralized network, allowing peer-to-peer transactions without intermediaries.
Blockchain
A public, decentralized ledger that records all Bitcoin transactions in chronological order. Each block contains a list of transactions, and blocks are linked together, forming a chain that ensures the integrity and security of data.
